Context

I work at Beyond, a SaaS company that helps short-term rental hosts and property managers across the world optimise their pricing and revenue using dynamic algorithms and market data. As a Strategic Customer Success Manager, I own a portfolio of high-value clients in Spain and Europe — helping them get the most out of the platform, growing their accounts over time, and making sure they stay.

The story
2024

Mastered the role from day one.

I moved from a Sales role into Customer Success — a fundamentally different discipline that requires building long-term client relationships, protecting existing revenue, and growing accounts over time rather than closing new ones. In year one, I adapted fast. I ended the year with zero revenue lost to churn, an average Net Revenue Retention above 108%, and strong expansion numbers in the second half. When key accounts were approached by direct competitors offering lower prices, I retained them through a consultative approach — making the case for value and outcomes rather than competing on cost. I also redesigned how I managed renewals, closing them significantly ahead of schedule to free capacity for growth work.

2025

Built the team around me.

The numbers scaled: 109.48% NRR, 37% of the team's total expansion revenue as a single contributor, #1 upgrade performer globally for the second consecutive year. But the more important story is what happened around those results. I mentored a junior teammate entering a complex market; they're now independently managing sophisticated accounts. My approach to early renewals was recognised by my manager as a model the wider team adopted. My manager wrote: "The team operates differently than they did 12 months ago — Alejo helped drive this transition." I delivered all of this while navigating new parenthood. The year ended with a promotion and President's Club recognition.

Next

Formalise what's already happening.

The gaps I identified as an individual contributor — building scalable strategies, formalising playbooks, enabling the team systematically — are precisely what I want to take on in a leadership role. I've been operating at the scope and impact of a manager from an IC seat. The next step is making it official.

Selected work

Four moments that define the case

01

Elevating the team's commercial approach

S The Customer Success team needed to shift from a reactive account management posture to a more commercially proactive one — expanding accounts earlier, generating new business, and being more visible with clients. A Led by example across two consecutive years — consistently #1 in expansion revenue, new business, and win-backs within the team. Shared methods openly and operated as an informal reference point for colleagues navigating complex commercial situations. R Manager noted a visible shift in how the whole team operated, attributing it directly to this influence: "His accomplishments have helped motivate the team to close deals early, be more customer facing, and overall level up their commercial muscle."
Team-wide behavioural change
02

Mentoring new joiners across EMEA

S As the team grew across EMEA, new joiners needed to ramp quickly on complex, high-value accounts — markets that require deep regional knowledge, consultative skills, and the ability to manage sophisticated clients from day one. A Acted as an informal mentor to multiple new team members — sharing account management frameworks, expansion strategies, and regional market knowledge built over years in the field. Became the go-to resource for colleagues navigating complex client situations, without reducing my own commercial targets. R Manager credited the mentorship directly in the year-end review: "We can already see the fruits of this mentorship as they take on more complex accounts and continue to build relationships with clients successfully."
Team-wide ramp acceleration
03

Retaining key accounts against direct competitors

S Several high-value clients were approached by direct competitors offering lower fees. Competing on price alone was not an option — it would have undermined the value proposition for the entire portfolio. A Developed tailored success plans for each at-risk account. Ran fully consultative retention conversations — quantifying the revenue outcomes the platform delivered versus what a cheaper alternative might cost them in performance. R All at-risk accounts retained. Clients chose to stay despite lower-cost alternatives, reinforcing confidence in the platform's value rather than its price. Contributed to a near-zero churn result for the full year.
99.76% Gross Revenue Retention
04

Designing a renewal model the team adopted

S Customer Success teams typically manage contract renewals reactively — close to the expiry date — which creates revenue uncertainty and leaves little room for proactive growth work. A Built and refined a proactive renewal approach over two years: closing contracts significantly ahead of expiry, securing multi-year agreements where possible, and framing early closures as a forecasting and capacity tool — not just a personal metric. Made the reasoning visible so it could be replicated by others. R Manager independently recognised the approach as a model for the team: "A model for how early execution creates bandwidth for higher-value activities." Freed up capacity that directly contributed to new business and win-back performance in the same period.
Team-adopted process
